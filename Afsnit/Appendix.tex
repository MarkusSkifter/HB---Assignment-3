\textbf{Symbol} & \textbf{Represents} \\
\midrule
$T$            & Length of the life cycle (number of periods the household lives) \\
$\rho$         & Time discount factor: how much future utility is worth today \\
$r$            & Interest rate on savings $a_t$ \\
$\eta$         & Curvature of utility from consumption (risk aversion / EIS) \\
$\gamma$       & Curvature of disutility from working hours \\
$\beta_0$      & Baseline weight on labor disutility (no child present) \\
$\beta_1$      & Extra labor disutility when a child is present in the household \\
$\bar{w}$      & Common pre-tax wage level (efficiency-units price of labor) \\
$\tau$         & Proportional labor income tax rate \\
$\alpha$       & Wage return to one unit of human capital \\
$\delta_1$     & Father's human capital depreciation rate while on leave \\
$\delta_2$     & Mother's human capital depreciation rate while on leave \\
$p_b$          & Probability of a birth in a given period when childless \\
$\bar{L}$      & Total parental leave time available to the household per birth \\
$\phi$         & Replacement rate of wages while on parental leave \\
$\bar{q}$      & Policy instrument: minimum share of leave the father must take \\
\midrule
$c_t$          & Household consumption in period $t$ \\
$h_{i,t}$      & Hours worked by member $i$ in period $t$ \\
$l_{i,t}$      & Share of period $t$ that member $i$ spends on parental leave \\
$\lambda_t$    & Father's share of total leave at the period of birth \\
$K_{i,t}$      & Human capital stock of member $i$ at the start of period $t$ \\
$w_{i,t}$      & After-tax wage of member $i$ in period $t$ \\
$a_t$          & Household assets at the start of period $t$ \\
$n_t$          & Indicator for the presence of a child ($n_t \in \{0,1\}$) \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
