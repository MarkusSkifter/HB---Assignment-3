\begin{abstract}
\noindent
Mothers in Denmark take roughly 80 percent of total parental leave, generating a persistent gender wage gap through reduced human capital accumulation. This paper proposes a two-earner finite-horizon life-cycle model to evaluate mandatory paternity leave quotas as a policy tool for closing that gap. The model features endogenous hours, asymmetric human capital depreciation during leave, and a policy constraint on the father's minimum leave share. Counterfactual simulations across quota levels trace the welfare trade-off between long-run wage convergence and short-run household income loss. The structural parameters can be estimated from Danish administrative data using simulated method of moments.
\end{abstract}
